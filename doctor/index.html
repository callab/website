about our research

Young children learn the meanings of thousands of words by the time they can run down the street. Yet, the same children who solve this problem constatnly forget where they left their coats and hats. At the Communication and Learning Lab, we use a variety of methods to investigate the language learning problem from the perspective of coordination.

How do children learn language so quickly despite cognitive constraints on memory, attention, and information processing?

How might parents, peers, and caretakers­­ coordinate their speech to children's learning mechanisms over early development? Do we talk to children in a way that optimizes their learning?

People

Daniel Yurovsky, Prinicipal Investigator

Website | Email

Dan is interested in how children’s rapid language acquisition emerges from the interaction of their learning mechanisms and the structure of their learning environments. His work combines behavioral and computational analysis of infants’, children’s, and adults’ learning mechanisms with corpus analyses of the language children hear and the world they see.

Ben Morris | Lab Manager

Email

Ben received his master's in Developmental Psychology at the University of Cambridge working with Dr. Claire Hughes. Ben has a bachelors in Psychology from Reed College. He is primarily interested optimal word learning strategies and their connection to early cognitive development.

For Parents


Thank you so much for your interest in our research! We rely on parents like you to help us answer our questions about children’s reasoning about the world.

If you are interested in participating in our research, please click here or call 555-555-5555.


How long do studies usually last?

We usually schedule for one hour, however appointments typicallly last for less than 30 minutes.

What if my child becomes fussy or bored during the study?

Involvement in research is entirely voluntary. Your child can stop at any time.

Will I be with my child during the study?

If you wish, you may stay with your child during the entire study.



Can I bring my other children with me?

Absolutely! We have a comfortable waiting room with plenty of toys to play with!

prospective students



We are always looking for motivated, passionate students and researchers to join our team. Please contact us if you have any questions about our work!


Undergraduate Opportunities: The Communication and Learning Lab has openings for undergraduates to gain research experience working as RAs. As a research assistant in the CaL Lab, you could be involved in research at every step of the process, from developing reserach questions and creating stimuli to recruiting subjects and testing participants. If you would like to hear more about working as an RA, please contact our lab manager Benjamin Morris (benmorris [at] uchicago [dot] edu).
