about our research

Young children learn the meanings of thousands of words by the time they can run down the street. Yet, the same children who solve this problem constatnly forget where they left their coats and hats.

How do children learn language so quickly despite cognitive constraints on memory, attention, and information processing?

How might parents, peers, and caretakers­­ coordinate their speech to children's learning mechanisms over early development? Do we talk to children in a way that optimizes their learning?

prospective students


We are always looking for motivated, passionate students and researchers to join our team. Please contact us if you have any questions about our work!

The Communication and Learning Lab offers research opportunities for undergraduates. We expect that our undergraduates be involved in all aspects of our research, from creating stimuli and recruiting subjects to testing participants, analyzing data, and formulating new research questions. Have questions about joining the CaL Lab?

Please contact our lab manager Benjamin Morris (benmorris [at] uchicago [dot] edu) for more information.
